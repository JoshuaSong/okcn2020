The Fall of Jerusalem Recounted
1#For ver. 1-27Zedekiah was twenty-one years old when he became king; and he reigned eleven years in Jerusalem. His mother's name was Hamutal the daughter of Jeremiah of Libnah. 2And he did what was evil in the sight of the Lord, #2 Kgs. 23:37See ch. 22:13-17according to all that Jehoiakim had done. 3For because of the anger of the Lord things came to the point in Jerusalem and Judah that he cast them out from his presence.
And Zedekiah rebelled against the king of Babylon. 4#For ver. 4-16And in the ninth year of his reign, in the tenth month, on the tenth day of the month, Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon came with all his army against Jerusalem, and laid siege to it. And they built siegeworks all around it. 5So the city was besieged till the eleventh year of King Zedekiah. 6On the ninth day of the fourth month the famine was so severe in the city that there was no food for the people of the land. 7Then a breach was made in the city, and all the men of war fled and went out from the city by night by the way of a gate between the two walls, by the king's garden, while the Chaldeans were around the city. And they went in the direction of the Arabah. 8But the army of the Chaldeans pursued the king and overtook Zedekiah in the plains of Jericho. And all his army was scattered from him. 9Then they captured the king and brought him up to the king of Babylon at Riblah in the land of Hamath, and he passed sentence on him. 10The king of Babylon slaughtered the sons of Zedekiah before his eyes, and also slaughtered all the officials of Judah at #ver. 26Riblah. 11#[Ezek. 12:13]He put out the eyes of Zedekiah, and bound him in chains, and the king of Babylon took him to Babylon, and put him in prison #[Ezek. 12:13]till the day of his death.
The Temple Burned
12#ch. 1:3In the fifth month, on #[2 Kgs. 25:8]the tenth day of the month—that was #[ver. 29]the nineteenth year of King Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon—Nebuzaradan the captain of the bodyguard, who #ch. 40:10served the king of Babylon, entered Jerusalem. 13And he burned the house of the Lord, and the king's house and all the houses of Jerusalem; every great house he burned down. 14And all the army of the Chaldeans, who were with the captain of the guard, broke down all the walls around Jerusalem. 15And Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carried away captive some of the poorest of the people and the rest of the people who were left in the city and #See ch. 37:13the deserters who had deserted to the king of Babylon, together with the rest of the artisans. 16But Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard left some of the poorest of the land to be vinedressers and plowmen.
17And the #ch. 27:19See 2 Chr. 4:12-15pillars of bronze that were in the house of the Lord, and the stands and the #2 Chr. 4:2bronze sea that were in the house of the Lord, the Chaldeans broke in pieces, and #See ch. 27:22carried all the bronze to Babylon. 18And they took away #2 Kgs. 25:14the pots and the shovels and the snuffers and the basins and the dishes for incense and all the vessels of bronze used in the temple service; 19#1 Kgs. 7:502 Kgs. 25:15also the small bowls and the fire pans and the basins and the pots and #1 Kgs. 7:49the lampstands and #Ex. 25:29|NoBook| 37:16the dishes for incense #Ex. 25:29|NoBook| 37:16and the bowls for drink offerings. What was of gold the captain of the guard took away as gold, and what was of silver, as silver. 20As for the two pillars, the one sea, #1 Kgs. 7:25the twelve bronze bulls that were under the sea,#Hebrew lacks the sea and the stands, which Solomon the king had made for the house of the Lord, the bronze of all these things was beyond weight. 21As for the pillars, the height of the one pillar was eighteen cubits,#A cubit was about 18 inches or 45 centimeters #1 Kgs. 7:15its circumference was twelve cubits, and its thickness was four fingers, and it was hollow. 22On it was a capital of bronze. And the second pillar had the same, with pomegranates. 23There were ninety-six pomegranates on the sides; all the pomegranates were a hundred upon the network all around.
The People Exiled to Babylon
24And the captain of the guard took #[1 Chr. 6:14Seraiah the chief priest, and #[ch. 29:25]Zephaniah the second priest, and the three keepers of the threshold; 25and from the city he took an officer who had been in command of the men of war, and #[Esth. 1:14]seven men of the king's council, who were found in the city; and the secretary of the commander of the army who mustered the people of the land; and sixty men of the people of the land, who were found in the midst of the city. 26And Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard took them and brought them to the king of Babylon at #ver. 9Riblah. 27And the king of Babylon struck them down, and put them to death at #[See ver. 26 above]Riblah in the land of Hamath. So Judah was taken into exile out of its land.
28This is the number of the people whom Nebuchadnezzar carried away captive: #[2 Kgs. 24:12in the seventh year, 3,023 Judeans; 29#[ver. 12]in the eighteenth year of Nebuchadnezzar he carried away captive from Jerusalem 832 persons; 30in the twenty-third year of Nebuchadnezzar, Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carried away captive of the Judeans 745 persons; all the persons were 4,600.
Jehoiachin Released from Prison
31#For ver. 31-34And in the thirty-seventh year of the exile of Jehoiachin king of Judah, in the twelfth month, on the twenty-fifth day of the month, Evil-merodach king of Babylon, in the year that he became king, graciously freed#Hebrew king, lifted up the head of #ch. 37:1See ch. 22:24-30Jehoiachin king of Judah and brought him out of prison. 32And he spoke kindly to him, and gave him a seat above the seats of #[ch. 27:3]the kings who were with him in Babylon. 33So Jehoiachin put off his prison garments. And every day of his life he dined regularly at the king's table, 34and for his allowance, a regular allowance was given him by the king according to his daily need, until the day of his death, as long as he lived.
