μάστιξ
a whip, scourge
μάστιξ a whip (literally, the Roman flagellum for criminals; figuratively, a disease)
Derivation: probably from the base of G3145 (through the idea of contact);
KJV Usage: plague, scourging. G3145
Thayer:
1) a whip, scourge
2) metaph. a scourge, plague
2a) a calamity, misfortune, esp. sent by God to discipline or punish
