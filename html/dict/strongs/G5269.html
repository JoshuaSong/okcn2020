ὑποζώννυμι
to undergird
ὑποζώννυμι to gird under, i.e. frap (a vessel with cables across the keel, sides and deck)
Derivation: from G5259 and G2224;
KJV Usage: undergirt. G5259 G2224
Thayer:
1) to undergird
1a) to bind a ship together laterally: i.e. with girths or cables, to enable it to survive the force of waves and tempest
