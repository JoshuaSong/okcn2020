ἐκζητέω
to seek out, enquire
ἐκζητέω to search out, i.e. (figuratively)investigate, crave, demand, (by Hebraism) worship
Derivation: from G1537 and G2212;
KJV Usage: en- (re-)quire, seek after (carefully, diligently). G1537 G2212
Thayer:
1) to seek out, search for
2) to seek out, i.e. investigate, scrutinise
3) to seek out for one's self, beg, crave
4) to demand back, require
