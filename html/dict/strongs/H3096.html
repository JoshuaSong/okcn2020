יהצה יהצה יהץ
yahats yahtsâh yahtsâh
yah'-hats, yah'-tsaw, yah-tsaw'
From an unused root meaning to stamp; perhaps threshing floor; Jahats or Jahtsah, a place East of the Jordan
KJV Usage: Jahaz, Jahazah, Jahzah.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

יהצה יהצה יהץ
Jahaz or Jahazah or Jahzah = "trodden down"
1. a place in Reuben east of the Dead Sea where Israel defeated Sihon king of the Amorites; site uncertain
Origin: from an unused root meaning to stamp
TWOT: None
