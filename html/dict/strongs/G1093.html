γῆ
earth
γῆ soil; by extension a region, or the solid part or the whole of the terrene globe (including the occupants in each application)
Derivation: contracted from a primary word;
KJV Usage: country, earth(-ly), ground, land, world.
Thayer:
1) arable land
2) the ground, the earth as a standing place
3) the main land as opposed to the sea or water
4) the earth as a whole
4a) the earth as opposed to the heavens
4b) the inhabited earth, the abode of men and animals
5) a country, land enclosed within fixed boundaries, a tract of land, territory, region
