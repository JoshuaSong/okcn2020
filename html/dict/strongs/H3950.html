לקט
lâqaṭ
law-kat'
A primitive root; properly to pick up, that is, (generally) to gather; specifically to glean
KJV Usage: gather (up), glean.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

לקט

1. to pick up, gather, glean, gather up
a. (Qal)
1. to pick up, gather
2. to glean
b. (Piel)
1. to gather, gather up
2. to collect (money)
3. to glean
c. (Pual) to be picked up
d. (Hithpael) to collect oneself
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1125
Parts of Speech: Verb
