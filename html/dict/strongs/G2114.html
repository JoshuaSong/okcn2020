εὐθυμέω
to be of good cheer
εὐθυμέω to cheer up, i.e. (intransitively) be cheerful; neuter comparative (adverbially) more cheerfully
Derivation: from G2115;
KJV Usage: be of good cheer (merry). G2115
Thayer:
1) to put in good spirits, gladden, make cheerful
1a) to be of good spirits, to be cheerful
2) to be joyful, be of good cheer, of good courage
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Cheer, Cheerful, Cheerfully, Cheerfulness
  • Merry (to be, to make)
