אמרי
'ĕmôrı̂y
em-o-ree'
Probably a patronymic from an unused name derived from H559 in the sense of publicity, that is, prominence; thus a mountaineer; an Emorite, one of the Canaanitish tribes
KJV Usage: Amorite.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

אמרי
Amorite = "a sayer"
1. one of the peoples of east Canaan and beyond the Jordan, dispossessed by the Israelite incursion from Egypt
Origin: probably a patronymic from an unused name derived from H559 in the sense of publicity, i.e. prominence
TWOT: 119
Parts of Speech: Noun Masculine
