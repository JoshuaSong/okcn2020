קשׁב
qâshab
kaw-shab'
A primitive root; to prick up the ears, that is, hearken
KJV Usage: attend, (cause to) hear (-ken), give heed, incline, mark (well), regard.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קשׁב

1. to hear, be attentive, heed, incline (of ears), attend (of ears), hearken, pay attention, listen
a. (Qal) incline, attend (of ears), hearken, pay attention, listen
b. (Hiphil) to pay attention, give attention
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2084
Parts of Speech: Verb
