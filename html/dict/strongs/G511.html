ἀνώτερος
higher
ἀνώτερος upper, i.e. (neuter as adverb) to a more conspicuous place, in a former part of the book
Derivation: comparative degree of G507;
KJV Usage: above, higher. G507
Thayer:
1) higher
1a) of motion: to a higher place, up higher
1b) of rest: in a higher place, above
