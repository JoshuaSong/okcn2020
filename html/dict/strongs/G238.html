ἀλληγορέω
to speak so as to imply something other than what is said, to interpret allegorically
ἀλληγορέω to allegorize
Derivation: from G243 and ἀγορέω (to harangue (compare G58));
KJV Usage: be an allegory (the Greek word itself). G243 G58
Thayer:
1) to speak allegorically or in a figure
