προγινώσκω to know beforehand, i.e. foresee
Derivation: from G4253 and G1097;
KJV Usage: foreknow (ordain), know (before). G4253 G1097
Thayer:
1) to have knowledge before hand
2) to foreknow
2a) of those whom God elected to salvation
3) to predestinate
