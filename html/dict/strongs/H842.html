אשׁירה אשׁרה
'ăshêrâh 'ăshêyrâh
ash-ay-raw', ash-ay-raw'
From H833; happy; asherah (or Astarte) a Phoenician goddess; also an image of the same
KJV Usage: grove. Compare H6253.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

אשׁירה אשׁרה
Ashera(h) = "groves (for idol worship)"
1. a Babylonian (Astarte)-Canaanite goddess (of fortune and happiness), the supposed consort of Baal, her images
a. the goddess, goddesses
b. her images
c. sacred trees or poles set up near an altar
Origin: from H833
TWOT: 183h
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Feminine
