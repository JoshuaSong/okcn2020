αὐχμηρός
dry, dusty, rough, squalid
αὐχμηρός properly, dirty, i.e. (by implication) obscure
Derivation: from αὐχμός (probably from a base akin to that of G109) (dust, as dried by wind);
KJV Usage: dark. G109
Thayer:
1) squalid, dirty
1a) since dirty things are destitute of brightness: dark
