ὀστράκινος
earthen, of clay
ὀστράκινος earthen-ware, i.e. clayey; by implication, frail
Derivation: from ὄστρακον ("oyster") (a tile, i.e. terra cotta);
KJV Usage: of earth, earthen.
Thayer:
1) earthen, clay
1a) with the added suggestion of frailty
