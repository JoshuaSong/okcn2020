שׁבח
shâbach
shaw-bakh'
A primitive root; properly to address in a loud tone, that is, (specifically) loud; figuratively to pacify (as if by words)
KJV Usage: commend, glory, keep in, praise, still, triumph.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁבח

1. to soothe, still, stroke
a. (Piel) to soothe, still
b. (Hiphil) stilling (participle)
2. to laud, praise, commend
a. (Piel)
1. to laud, praise (God)
2. to commend, congratulate (the dead)
b. (Hithpael) to boast
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2312,2313
Parts of Speech: Verb
