סיסרא
sı̂yse râ'
see-ser-aw'
Of uncertain derivation; Sisera, the name of a Canaanitish king and of one of the Nethinim
KJV Usage: Sisera.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

סיסרא
Sisera = "battle array"
1. the conquering general under king Jabin of Hazor and slain by Jael
2. ancestor of a family of temple slaves who returned from exile with Zerubbabel
Origin: of uncertain derivation
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
