σίναπι
mustard
σίναπι mustard (the plant)
Derivation: perhaps from σίνομαι (to hurt, i.e. sting);
KJV Usage: mustard.
Thayer:
1) mustard, the name of a plant which in oriental countries grows from a very small seed and attains to the height of a tree,
10 feet (3 m) and more; hence a very small quantity of a thing is likened to a mustard seed, and also a thing which grows to a remarkable size
