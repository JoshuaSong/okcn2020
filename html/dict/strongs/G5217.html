ὑπάγω
to lead
ὑπάγω to lead (oneself) under, i.e. withdraw or retire (as if sinking out of sight), literally or figuratively
Derivation: from G5259 and G71;
KJV Usage: depart, get hence, go (a-)way. G5259 G71
Thayer:
1) to lead under, bring under
2) to withdraw one's self, to go away, depart
