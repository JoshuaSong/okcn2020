צלצל
tse lâtsal
tsel-aw-tsal'
From H6750 reduplicated; a clatter, that is, (abstractly) whirring (of wings); (concretely) a cricket; also a harpoon (as rattling), a cymbal (as clanging)
KJV Usage: cymbal, locust, shadowing, spear.
