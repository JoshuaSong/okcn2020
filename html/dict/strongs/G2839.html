κοινός
common, shared in common
κοινός common, i.e. (literally) shared by all or several, or (ceremonially) profane
Derivation: probably from G4862;
KJV Usage: common, defiled, unclean, unholy. G4862
Thayer:
1) common
2) common i.e. ordinary, belonging to generality
2a) by the Jews, unhallowed, profane, Levitically unclean
