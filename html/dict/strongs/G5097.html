τιμωρέω
to help, aid, succour
τιμωρέω properly, to protect one's honor, i.e. to avenge (inflict a penalty)
Derivation: from a comparative of G5092 and οὖρος ouros (a guard);
KJV Usage: punish. G5092
Thayer:
1) to be a guardian or avenger of honour
1a) to succour, come to the help of
2) to avenge
3) in the NT, to take vengeance on one, to punish
