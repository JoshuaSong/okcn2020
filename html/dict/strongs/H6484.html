פּצר
pâtsar
paw-tsar'
A primitive root; to peck at, that is, (figuratively) stun or dull
KJV Usage: press, urge, stubbornness.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

פּצר

1. to press, push
a. (Qal) to push, press
b. (Hiphil) be insolent, display pushing (arrogance, presumption)
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1801
Parts of Speech: Verb
