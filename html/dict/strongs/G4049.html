περισπάω
to draw off from around, to strip off
περισπάω to drag all around, i.e. (figuratively) to distract (with care)
Derivation: from G4012 and G4685;
KJV Usage: cumber. G4012 G4685
Thayer:
1) to draw around, to draw away, distract
2) metaph.
2a) to be driven about mentally, to be distracted
2b) to be over-occupied, too busy, about a thing
