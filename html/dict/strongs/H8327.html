שׁרשׁ
shârash
shaw-rash'
A primitive root; to root, that is, strike into the soil, or (by implication) to pluck from it
KJV Usage: (take, cause to take) root (out).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁרשׁ

1. to uproot, take root, deal with the roots
a. (Piel) to root up, root out
b. (Pual) to be rooted up or out (of produce)
c. (Poel) to take root
d. (Poal) to take root
e. (Hiphil) to take root, cause to take root
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2471
Parts of Speech: Verb
