ὀψέ
after a long time, late
ὀψέ (adverbially) late in the day; by extension, after the close of the day
Derivation: from the same as G3694 (through the idea of backwardness);
KJV Usage: (at) even, in the end. G3694
Thayer:
1) after a long time, long after, late
1a) late in the day, i.e. at evening
1b) the sabbath having just passed, after the sabbath
1b1) at the early dawn of the first day of the week
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Even (Noun), Evening, Eventide
  • Late
