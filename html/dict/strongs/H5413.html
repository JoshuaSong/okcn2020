נתך
nâthak
naw-thak'
A primitive root; to flow forth (literally or figuratively); by implication to liquefy
KJV Usage: drop, gather (together), melt, pour (forth, out).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

נתך

1. to pour out or forth, drop (or rain), be poured, be poured out, be melted, be molten
a. (Qal) to pour forth
b. (Niphal) to be poured, be poured out
c. (Hiphil) to pour out, melt
d. (Hophal) to be melted
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1442
Parts of Speech: Verb
