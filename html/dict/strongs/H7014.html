קין
qayin
kah'-yin
The same as H7013 (with a play upon the affinity to H7069); Kajin, the name of the first child, also of a place in Palestine, and of an Oriental tribe
KJV Usage: Cain, Kenite (-s).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קין
n pr m Cain = "possession"
1. eldest son of Adam and Eve and the first murderer having murdered his brother Abel n pr gent Kenite = "smiths"
2. the tribe from which the father-in-law of Moses was a member and which lived in the area between southern Palestine and the mountains of Sinai
Origin: the same as H7013 (with a play upon the affinity to H7069)
TWOT: 2017,2016
