χείρ
the hand
χείρ the hand (literally or figuratively (power); especially (by Hebraism) a means or instrument)
Derivation: perhaps from the base of G5494 in the sense of its congener the base of G5490 (through the idea of hollowness for grasping);
KJV Usage: hand. G5494 G5490
Thayer:
1) by the help or agency of any one, by means of any one
2) fig. applied to God symbolising his might, activity, power
2a) in creating the universe
2b) in upholding and preserving (God is present protecting and aiding one)
2c) in punishing
2d) in determining and controlling the destinies of men
