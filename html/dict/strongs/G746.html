ἀρχή
a beginning, origin, first cause
ἀρχή (properly abstract) a commencement, or (concretely) chief (in various applications of order, time, place, or rank)
Derivation: from G756;
KJV Usage: beginning, corner, (at the, the) first (estate), magistrate, power, principality, principle, rule. G756
Thayer:
1) beginning, origin
2) the person or thing that commences, the first person or thing in a series, the leader
3) that by which anything begins to be, the origin, the active cause
4) the extremity of a thing
4a) of the corners of a sail
5) the first place, principality, rule, magistracy
5a) of angels and demons
