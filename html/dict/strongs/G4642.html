σκληρός
hard
σκληρός dry, i.e. hard or tough (figuratively, harsh, severe)
Derivation: from the base of G4628;
KJV Usage: fierce, hard. G4628
Thayer:
1) hard, harsh, rough, stiff
1a) of men: metaph. harsh, stern, hard
1b) of things: violent, rough, offensive, intolerable
Berry's New Testament Synonyms
  • Harsh, austere
Trench's New Testament Synonyms
  • Hard
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Hard, Harden, Hardening, Hardness
