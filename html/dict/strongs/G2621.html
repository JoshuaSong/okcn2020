κατάκειμαι
to lie down, lie outstretched
κατάκειμαι to lie down, i.e. (by implication) be sick; specially, to recline at a meal
Derivation: from G2596 and G2749;
KJV Usage: keep, lie, sit at meat (down). G2596 G2749
Thayer:
1) to have lain down, i.e. to lie prostrate
1a) of the sick
1b) of those at meals, to recline
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Lie (to lie down, on, upon)
  • Sit
