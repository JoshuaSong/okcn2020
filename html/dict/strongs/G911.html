βάπτω
to dip in water
βάπτω to whelm, i.e. cover wholly with a fluid; in the New Testament only in a qualified or special sense, i.e. (literally) to moisten (a part of one's person), or (by implication) to stain (as with dye)
Derivation: a primary verb;
KJV Usage: dip.
Thayer:
1) to dip, dip in, immerse
2) to dip into dye, to dye, colour
