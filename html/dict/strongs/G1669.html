ἑλκόω
to wound sorely, lacerate
ἑλκόω to cause to ulcerate, i.e. (passively) be ulcerous
Derivation: from G1668;
KJV Usage: full of sores. G1668
Thayer:
1) to make sore, cause to ulcerate
2) to be ulcerated
3) full of sores
