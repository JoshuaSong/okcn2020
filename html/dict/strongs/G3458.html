μύλος
a millstone
μύλος a "mill", i.e. (by implication), a grinder (millstone)
Derivation: probably ultimately from the base of G3433 (through the idea of hardship);
KJV Usage: millstone. G3433
Thayer:
1) a mill stone
1a) a large mill consisted of two stones, an upper and an under one
1b) the "nether" stone was stationary, but the upper one was turned by an ass
2) a mill, the noise made by a mill
