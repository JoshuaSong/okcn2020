תּוּר
tûr
toor
A primitive root; to meander (causatively guide) about, especially for trade or reconnoitring
KJV Usage: chap [-man], sent to descry, be excellent, merchant [-man], search (out), seek, (e-) spy (out).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

תּוּר

1. to seek, search out, spy out, explore
a. (Qal)
1. to seek out, select, find out how to do something
2. to spy out, explore 1a
b. explorers, spies (participle)
1. to go about 1a
c. merchant, trader (participle)
d. (Hiphil) to make a search, make a reconnaissance
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2500
Parts of Speech: Verb
