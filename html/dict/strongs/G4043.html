περιπατέω
to walk up and down, to walk about
περιπατέω to tread all around, i.e. walk at large (especially as proof of ability); figuratively, to live, deport oneself, follow (as a companion or votary)
Derivation: from G4012 and G3961;
KJV Usage: go, be occupied with, walk (about). G4012 G3961
Thayer:
1) to walk
1a) to make one's way, progress; to make due use of opportunities
1b) Hebrew for, to live
1b1) to regulate one's life
1b2) to conduct one's self
1b3) to pass one's life
