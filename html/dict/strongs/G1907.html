ἐπέχω
to have
ἐπέχω to hold upon, i.e. (by implication) to retain; (by extension) to detain; (with implication, of G3563) to pay attention to
Derivation: from G1909 and G2192;
KJV Usage: give (take) heed unto, hold forth, mark, stay. G1909 G2192 G3563
Thayer:
1) to have or hold upon, apply, to observe, attend to
1a) to give attention to
2) to hold towards, hold forth, present
3) to check
3a) delay, stop, stay
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words

  • Hold (down, fast, forth, on, to, up), Held, Holden, (take) Hold
  • Mark (Verb)
  • Stay
