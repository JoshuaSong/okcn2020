προσοχθίζω to feel indignant at
Derivation: from G4314 and a form of ὀχθέω (to be vexed with something irksome);
KJV Usage: be grieved at. G4314
Thayer:
1) to be wroth or displeased with
2) to loathe
3) to spew out
4) to be disgusted with
