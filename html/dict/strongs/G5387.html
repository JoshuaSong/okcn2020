φιλόστοργος
loving tenderly, affectionate
φιλόστοργος fond of natural relatives, i.e. fraternal towards fellow Christian
Derivation: from G5384 and στοργή (cherishing one's kindred, especially parents or children);
KJV Usage: kindly affectioned. G5384
Thayer:
1) the mutual love of parents and children and wives and husbands
2) loving affection, prone to love, loving tenderly
2a) chiefly of the reciprocal tenderness of parents and children
