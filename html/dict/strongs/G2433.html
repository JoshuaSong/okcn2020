ἱλάσκομαι
to appease
ἱλάσκομαι to conciliate, i.e. (transitively) to atone for (sin), or (intransitively) be propitious
Derivation: middle voice from the same as G2436;
KJV Usage: be merciful, make reconciliation for. G2436
Thayer:
1) to render one's self, to appease, conciliate to one's self
1a) to become propitious, be placated or appeased
1b) to be propitious, be gracious, be merciful
2) to expiate, make propitiation for
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Merciful (Adjective, and Verb, to be), Mercy (Noun, and Verb, to have, etc.)
  • Propitiation
