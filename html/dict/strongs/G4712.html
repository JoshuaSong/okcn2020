στάδιον
a stade, =
στάδιον , or masculine (in plural) στάδιος a stade or certain measure of distance; by implication, a stadium or race-course
Derivation: from the base of G2476 (as fixed);
KJV Usage: furlong, race. G2476
Thayer:
1) a space or distance of about
600 feet (185 m)
2) a race course
2a) place in which contests in running were held, the one who outstripped the rest and reached the goal first, receiving the prize. Courses of this description were found in most of the larger Greek cities, and were like that at Olympia, 600 Greek feet in length
