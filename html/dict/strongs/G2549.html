κακία
badness
κακία badness, i.e. (subjectively) depravity, or (actively) malignity, or (passively) trouble
Derivation: from G2556;
KJV Usage: evil, malice(-iousness), naughtiness, wickedness. G2556
Thayer:
1) malignity, malice, ill-will, desire to injure
2) wickedness, depravity
2a) wickedness that is not ashamed to break laws
3) evil, trouble
For Synonyms see entry G5855
