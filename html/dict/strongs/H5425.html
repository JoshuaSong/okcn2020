נתר
nâthar
naw-thar'
A primitive root; to jump, that is, be violently agitated; causatively, to terrify, shake off, untie
KJV Usage: drive asunder, leap, (let) loose, X make, move, undo.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

נתר

1. to start up, tremble, shake, spring up
a. (Qal) to start up
b. (Piel) to leap
c. (Hiphil) to cause to start up
2. to loose, let loose, undo, be free, be loose
a. (Hiphil)
1. to unfasten, loosen
2. to set free, unbind
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1448,1449
Parts of Speech: Verb
