סכר שׂכר
śâkar sâkar
saw-kar', saw-kar'
The second form by permutation and used in Ezra 4:5; a primitive root (apparently akin (by prosthesis) to H3739 through the idea of temporary purchase; compare H7937); to hire
KJV Usage: earn wages, hire (out self), reward, X surely.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

סכר שׂכר

1. to hire
a. (Qal) to hire
b. (Niphal) to hire oneself out
c. (Hithpael) to earn wages
Origin: a primitive root [apparently akin (by prosthesis) to H3739 through the idea of temporary purchase]
TWOT: 2264.1
Parts of Speech: Verb
