Παῦλος (little; but remotely from a derivative of G3973, meaning the same); Paulus, the name of a Roman and of an apostle
Derivation: of Latin origin;
KJV Usage: Paul, Paulus. G3973
Thayer:
1) Paul was the most famous of the apostles and wrote a good part of the NT, the
14 Pauline epistles 2) Paulus was a deputy or pro-consul of Cyprus and is said to be a prudent man, in the management of affairs, as a governor
Literal: Paul or Paulus = "small or little"
