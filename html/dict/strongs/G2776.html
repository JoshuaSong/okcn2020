κεφαλή
the head
κεφαλή the head (as the part most readily taken hold of), literally or figuratively
Derivation: from the primary κάπτω (in the sense of seizing);
KJV Usage: head.
Thayer:
1) the head, both of men and often of animals. Since the loss of the head destroys life, this word is used in the phrases relating to capital and extreme punishment.
2) metaph. anything supreme, chief, prominent
2a) of persons, master lord: of a husband in relation to his wife
2b) of Christ: the Lord of the husband and of the Church
2c) of things: the corner stone
