חטּוּשׁ
chaṭṭûsh
khat-toosh'
From an unused root of uncertain signification; Chattush, the name of four or five Israelites
KJV Usage: Hattush.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

חטּוּשׁ
Hattush = "assembled"
1. a descendant of David, apparently one of the sons of Shechaniah, in the 4th or 5th generation from Zerubbabel
a. a man who returned with Ezra; maybe same as 1 above
b. a man who returned with Zerubbabel; maybe same as 1 above
2. son of Hashabniah who helped repair the walls of Jerusalem with Nehemiah
Origin: from an unused root of uncertain signification
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
