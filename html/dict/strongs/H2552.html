חמם
châmam
khaw-mam'
A primitive root; to be hot (literally or figuratively)
KJV Usage: enflame self, get (have) heat, be (wax) hot, (be, wax) warm (self, at).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

חמם

1. to be hot, become warm
a. (Qal)
1. to be or grow warm
2. of passion (fig.)
b. (Niphal) to become aroused, inflame oneself with
c. (Piel) to warm
d. (Hithpael) to warm oneself
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: TWOT- 677
Parts of Speech: Verb
