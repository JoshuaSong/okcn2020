דּישׁ דּושׁ דּוּשׁ
dûsh dôsh dı̂ysh
doosh, dosh, deesh
A primitive root; to trample or thresh
KJV Usage: break, tear, thresh, tread out (down), at grass [Jer. H50 : H11, by mistake for H1877].

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

דּישׁ דּושׁ דּוּשׁ

1. to tread out, thresh
a. (Qal) to tread on, trample on, thresh
b. (Niphal) to be trampled down
c. (Hophal) to be threshed
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 419
Parts of Speech: Verb
