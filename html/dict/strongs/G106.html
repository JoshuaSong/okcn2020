ἄζυμος
unleavened
ἄζυμος unleavened, i.e. (figuratively) uncorrupted; (in the neutral plural) specially (by implication) the Passover week
Derivation: from G1 (as a negative particle) and G2219;
KJV Usage: unleavened (bread). G1 G2219
Thayer:
1) unfermented, free from leaven or yeast
1a) of the unleavened loaves used in the paschal feast of the Jews
1b) metaph. free from faults or the "leaven of iniquity"
