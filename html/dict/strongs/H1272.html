בּרח
bârach
baw-rakh'
A primitive root; to bolt, that is, figuratively to flee suddenly
KJV Usage: chase (away); drive away, fain, flee (away), put to flight, make haste, reach, run away, shoot.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

בּרח

1. to go through, flee, run away, chase, drive away, put to flight, reach, shoot (extend), hurry away
a. (Qal)
1. to go, pass through
2. to flee
3. to hasten, come quickly
b. (Hiphil)
1. to pass through
2. to cause to flee, put to flight
3. to drive away
Origin: a primitive root
