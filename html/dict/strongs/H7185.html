קשׁה
qâshâh
kaw-shaw'
A primitive root; properly to be dense, that is, tough or severe (in various applications)
KJV Usage: be cruel, be fiercer, make grievous, be ([ask a], be in, have, seem, would) hard (-en, [labour], -ly, thing), be sore, (be, make) stiff (-en, [-necked]).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קשׁה

1. to be hard, be severe, be fierce, be harsh
a. (Qal)
1. to be hard, be difficult
2. to be hard, be severe
b. (Niphal)
1. to be ill-treated
2. to be hard pressed
c. (Piel) to have severe labour (of women)
d. (Hiphil)
1. to make difficult, make difficulty
2. to make severe, make burdensome
3. to make hard, make stiff, make stubborn 1d
e. of obstinacy (fig)
1. to show stubbornness
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2085
Parts of Speech: Verb
