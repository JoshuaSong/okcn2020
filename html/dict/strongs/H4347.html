מכּה מכּה
makkâh makkeh
mak-kaw', mak-keh'
(Plural only) from H5221; a blow (in 2 Chronicles 2:10, of the flail); by implication a wound; figuratively carnage, also pestilence
KJV Usage: beaten, blow, plague, slaughter, smote, X sore, stripe, stroke, wound ([-ed]).
