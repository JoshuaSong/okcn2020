פּשׂק
pâśaq
paw-sak'
A primitive root; to dispart (the feet or lips), that is, become licentious
KJV Usage: open (wide).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

פּשׂק

1. to part, open wide
a. (Qal)
1. to open wide
2. to be talkative (of the mouth)
b. (Piel) to open wide
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1842
Parts of Speech: Verb
