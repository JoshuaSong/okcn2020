θριαμβεύω
to triumph
θριαμβεύω and a derivative of G680 (meaning a noisy iambus, sung in honor of Bacchus); to make an acclamatory procession, i.e. (figuratively) to conquer or (by Hebraism) to give victory
Derivation: from a prolonged compound of the base of G2360;
KJV Usage: (cause) to triumph (over). G2360 G680
Thayer:
1) to triumph, to celebrate a triumph
2) cause one to triumphFrom the root word meaning a hymn sung in festal processions in honour of the god Bacchus.
