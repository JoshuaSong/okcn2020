αἵρεσις
a taking especially
αἵρεσις properly, a choice, i.e. (specially) a party or (abstractly) disunion
Derivation: from G138;
KJV Usage: heresy (which is the Greek word itself), sect. G138
Thayer:
1) act of taking, capture: e.g. storming a city
2) choosing, choice
3) that which is chosen
4) a body of men following their own tenets (sect or party)
4a) of the Sadducees
4b) of the Pharisees
4c) of the Christians
5) dissensions arising from diversity of opinions and aims
