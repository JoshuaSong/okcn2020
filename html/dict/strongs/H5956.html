עלם
‛âlam
aw-lam'
A primitive root; to veil from sight, that is, conceal (literally or figuratively)
KJV Usage: X any ways, blind, dissembler, hide (self), secret (thing).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עלם

1. to conceal, hide, be hidden, be concealed, be secret
a. (Qal) secret (participle)
b. (Niphal)
1. to be concealed
2. concealed, dissembler (participle)
c. (Hiphil) to conceal, hide
d. (Hithpael) to hide oneself
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1629
Parts of Speech: Verb
