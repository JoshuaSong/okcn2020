ἐπιτίθημι
to lay, put
ἐπιτίθημι to impose (in a friendly or hostile sense)
Derivation: from G1909 and G5087;
KJV Usage: add unto, lade, lay upon, put (up) on, set on (up), + surname, X wound. G1909 G5087
Thayer:
1) in the active voice
1a) to put or lay upon
1b) to add to
2) in the middle voice
2a) to have put on, bid to be laid on
2b) to lay or throw one's self upon
2c) to attack one, to make an assault on one
