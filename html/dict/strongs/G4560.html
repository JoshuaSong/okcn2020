σάρκινος
of flesh, in the flesh
σάρκινος similar to flesh, i.e. (by analogy) soft
Derivation: from G4561;
KJV Usage: fleshly. G4561
Thayer:
1) fleshly, consisting of flesh, composed of flesh
2) pertaining to the body (as earthly and perishable material)
3) wholly given up to the flesh, rooted in the flesh as it were
Berry's New Testament Synonyms
  • Fleshly, fleshy, sensual
Trench's New Testament Synonyms
  • Carnal
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Carnal, Carnally
  • Fleshly, Fleshly
