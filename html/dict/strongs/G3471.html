μωραίνω
to be silly, foolish
μωραίνω to become insipid; figuratively, to make (passively, act) as a simpleton
Derivation: from G3474;
KJV Usage: become fool, make foolish, lose savour. G3474
Thayer:
1) to be foolish, to act foolishly
2a) to make foolish
2a1) to prove a person or a thing foolish
2b) to make flat and tasteless
2b1) of salt that has lost its strength and flavour
