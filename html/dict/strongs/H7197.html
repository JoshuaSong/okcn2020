קשׁשׁ
qâshash
kaw-shash'
A primitive root; to become sapless through drought; used only as denominative from H7179; to forage for straw, stubble or wood; figuratively to assemble
KJV Usage: gather (selves) (together).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קשׁשׁ

1. to gather, assemble, collect, gather stubble or sticks
a. (Qal) to gather together
b. (Poel) to gather stubble
c. (Hithpoel) to gather oneself together
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2091,2092
Parts of Speech: Verb
