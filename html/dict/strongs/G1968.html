ἐπιπίπτω
to fall upon
ἐπιπίπτω to embrace (with affection) or seize (with more or less violence; literally or figuratively)
Derivation: from G1909 and G4098;
KJV Usage: fall into (on, upon) lie on, press upon. G1909 G4098
Thayer:
1) to fall upon, to rush or press upon
1a) to lie upon one
1b) to fall into one's embrace
1c) to fall back upon
2) metaph.
2a) to fall upon one i.e. to seize, take possession of him
2a1) of the Holy Spirit, in his inspiration and impulse
2a2) of reproaches cast upon one
