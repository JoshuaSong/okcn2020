מעה
mê‛eh
may-aw'
From an unused root probably meaning to be soft; used only in plural the intestines, or (collectively) the abdomen, figuratively sympathy; by implication a vest; by extension the stomach, the uterus (or of men, the seat of generation), the heart (figuratively)
KJV Usage: belly, bowels, X heart, womb.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

מעה

1. internal organs, inward parts, bowels, intestines, belly
a. inward parts
b. digestive organs
c. organs of procreation, womb
d. place of emotions or distress or love (fig.)
e. external belly
Origin: from an unused root probably meaning to be soft
TWOT: 1227a
Parts of Speech: Noun Masculine
