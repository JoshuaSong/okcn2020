דּוּן דּין
dı̂yn dûn
deen, doon
A primitive root (compare H113); to rule; by implication to judge (as umpire); also to strive (as at law)
KJV Usage: contend, execute (judgment), judge, minister judgment, plead (the cause), at strife, strive.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

דּוּן דּין

1. to judge, contend, plead
a. (Qal)
1. to act as judge, minister judgment
2. to plead a cause
3. to execute judgment, requite, vindicate
4. to govern
5. to contend, strive
b. (Niphal) to be at strife, quarrel
Origin: a primitive root [comp. H113]
TWOT: 426
Parts of Speech: Verb
