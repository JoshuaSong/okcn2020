ṭarpe lay
tar-pel-ah'ee
(Chaldee); from a name of foreign derivation; a Tarpelite (collectively) or inhabitant of Tarpel, a place in Assyria
KJV Usage: Tarpelites.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

טרפּלי
Tarpelites = "they of the fallen (or wondrous) mountain"
1. the group of Assyrian colonists who were planted in the cities of Samaria after the captivity of the northern kingdom of Israel; identity uncertain
Origin: from a name of foreign derivation
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Noun Masculine
