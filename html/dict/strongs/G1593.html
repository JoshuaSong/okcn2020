ἐκνεύω
to turn the head aside
ἐκνεύω (by analogy) to slip off, i.e. quietly withdraw
Derivation: from G1537 and G3506;
KJV Usage: convey self away. G1537 G3506
Thayer:
1) to bend to one side
2) to take one's self away, to turn one's self, to avoid a thing
3) to swim away, escape by swimming
4) to escape, slip away secretly
