καθίζω
to make to sit down, seat
καθίζω to seat down, i.e. set (figuratively, appoint); intransitively, to sit (down); figuratively, to settle (hover, dwell)
Derivation: another (active) form for G2516;
KJV Usage: continue, set, sit (down), tarry. G2516
Thayer:
1) to make to sit down
1a) to set, appoint, to confer a kingdom on one
2) intransitively
2a) to sit down
2b) to sit
2b1) to have fixed one's abode
2b2) to sojourn, to settle, settle down
