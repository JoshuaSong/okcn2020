ἀσθενέω
to be weak, feeble, sickly
ἀσθενέω to be feeble (in any sense)
Derivation: from G772;
KJV Usage: be diseased, impotent folk (man), (be) sick, (be, be made) weak. G772
Thayer:
1) to be weak, feeble, to be without strength, powerless
2) to be weak in means, needy, poor
3) to be feeble, sick
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Disease, Diseased (be)
  • Impotent
  • Sick, Sickly, Sickness
  • Weak, Weakened, Weaker, Weakness
