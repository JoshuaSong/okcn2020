מסס
mâsas
maw-sas'
A primitive root; to liquefy; figuratively to waste (with disease), to faint (with fatigue, fear or grief)
KJV Usage: discourage, faint, be loosed, melt (away), refuse, X utterly.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

מסס

1. to dissolve, melt
a. (Qal) to waste away
b. (Niphal)
1. to melt, vanish, drop off, melt away
2. to faint, grow fearful (fig.)
3. wasted, worthless (participle)
c. (Hiphil) to cause to melt
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1223
Parts of Speech: Verb
