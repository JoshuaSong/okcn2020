ke rêthı̂y
ker-ay-thee'
Probably from H3772 in the sense of executioner; a Kerethite or life guardsman (compare H3876), (only collectively in the singular as plural)
KJV Usage: Cherethims, Cherethites.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּרתי
Cherethites = "executioners"
1. a group of foreign mercenary soldiers serving as a bodyguard for king David; also executioners
2. either Cretans or proto-Philistines (in general)
Origin: probably from H3772 in the sense of executioner
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Adjective
