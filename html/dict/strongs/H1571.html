גּם
gam
gam
By contraction from an unused root meaning to gather; properly assemblage; used only adverbially also, even, yea, though; often repeated as correlation both ... and
KJV Usage: again, alike, also, (so much) as (soon), both (so) . . . and, but, either . . . or, even, for all, (in) likewise (manner), moreover, nay . . . neither, one, then (-refore), though, what, with, yea.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

גּם

1. also, even, indeed, moreover, yea
a. also, moreover (giving emphasis)
b. neither, neither...nor (with negative)
c. even (for stress)
d. indeed, yea (introducing climax)
e. also (of correspondence or retribution)
f. but, yet, though (adversative)
g. even, yea, yea though (with 'when' in hypothetical case)
2. (TWOT) again, alike
Origin: by contraction from an unused root
TWOT: 361a
Parts of Speech: Adverb
