διώκω
to pursue
διώκω compare the base of G1169 and G1249); to pursue (literally or figuratively); by implication, to persecute
Derivation: a prolonged (and causative) form of a primary verb δίω (to flee;
KJV Usage: ensue, follow (after), given to, (suffer) persecute(-ion), press forward. G1169 G1249
Thayer:
1) to make to run or flee, put to flight, drive away
2) to run swiftly in order to catch a person or thing, to run after
2a) to press on: figuratively of one who in a race runs swiftly to reach the goal
2b) to pursue (in a hostile manner)
3) in any way whatever to harass, trouble, molest one
3a) to persecute
3b) to be mistreated, suffer persecution on account of something
4) without the idea of hostility, to run after, follow after: someone
5) metaph., to pursue
5a) to seek after eagerly, earnestly endeavour to acquire
