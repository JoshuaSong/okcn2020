קטן
qâṭôn
kaw-tone'
A primitive root (rather denominative from H6996); to diminish, that is, be (causatively make) diminutive or (figuratively) of no account
KJV Usage: be a (make) small (thing), be not worthy.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קטן

1. to be small, be insignificant
a. (Qal) to be insignificant
b. (Hiphil) to make small, reduce significantly
Origin: a primitive root [rather denominative from H6996]
TWOT: 2009
Parts of Speech: Verb
