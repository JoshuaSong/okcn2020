ὑγιαίνω
to be sound, healthy
to be sound, healthy
ὑγιαίνω to have sound health, i.e. be well (in body); figuratively, to be uncorrupt (true in doctrine)
Derivation: from G5199;
KJV Usage: be in health, (be safe and) sound, (be) whole(-some). G5199
Thayer:
1) to be sound, to be well, to be in good health
2) metaph.
2a) of Christians whose opinions are free from any mixture of error
2b) of one who keeps the graces and is strong
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Health (to be in)
  • Safe, Safely, Safety
  • Sound (Adjective), be Sound
  • Whole (made), Wholly, Wholesome
