פּצח
pâtsach
paw-tsakh'
A primitive root; to break out (in joyful sound)
KJV Usage: break (forth, forth into joy), make a loud noise.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

פּצח

1. to cause to break or burst forth, break forth with, break out
a. (Qal) to break out, break forth with
b. (Piel) to break up
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1796
Parts of Speech: Verb
