ἐξωθέω
to thrust out, force out, wrench out
ἐξωθέω or ἐξώθω to expel; by implication, to propel
Derivation: from G1537 and ὠθέω (to push);
KJV Usage: drive out, thrust in. G1537
Thayer:
1) to thrust out
2) expel from one's abode
3) to propel, drive
