ἐκπορεύομαι to depart, be discharged, proceed, project
Derivation: from G1537 and G4198;
KJV Usage: come (forth, out of), depart, go (forth, out), issue, proceed (out of). G1537 G4198
Thayer:
1) to go forth, go out, depart
2) metaph.
2a) to come forth, to issue, to proceed
2a1) of feelings, affections, deeds, sayings
2b) to flow forth
2b1) of a river
2c) to project, from the mouth of one
2d) to spread abroad, of a rumour
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Come, Came (see also Coming)
  • Depart
  • Issue
  • Proceed
