ὑποτάσσω
to place
ὑποτάσσω to subordinate; reflexively, to obey
Derivation: from G5259 and G5021;
KJV Usage: be under obedience (obedient), put under, subdue unto, (be, make) subject (to, unto), be (put) in subjection (to, under), submit self unto. G5259 G5021
Thayer:
1) to arrange under, to subordinate
2) to subject, put in subjection
3) to subject one's self, obey
4) to submit to one's control
5) to yield to one's admonition or advice
6) to obey, be subject
