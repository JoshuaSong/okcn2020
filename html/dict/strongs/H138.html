אדניּהוּ אדניּה
'ădônı̂yâh 'ădônı̂yâhû
ad-o-nee-yaw', ad-o-nee-yaw'-hoo
From H113 and H3050; lord (that is, worshipper) of Jah; Adonijah, the name of three Israelites
KJV Usage: Adonijah.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

אדניּהוּ אדניּה
Adonijah = "my lord is Jehovah"
1. fourth son of David and Solomon's rival for the throne
2. Levite sent by Jehoshaphat to teach the Law
3. a chief of the people who co-operated with Nehemiah
Origin: from H113 and H3050
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
