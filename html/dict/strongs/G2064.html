ἔρχομαι
to come
ἔρχομαι to come or go (in a great variety of applications, literally and figuratively)
Derivation: middle voice of a primary verb (used only in the present and imperfect tenses, the others being supplied by a kindred (middle voice) ἐλεύθομαι , or (active) ἔλθω , which do not otherwise occur);
KJV Usage: accompany, appear, bring, come, enter, fall out, go, grow, X light, X next, pass, resort, be set.
Thayer:
1) to come
1a) of persons
1a1) to come from one place to another, and used both of persons arriving
1a2) to appear, make one's appearance, come before the public
2) metaph.
2a) to come into being, arise, come forth, show itself, find place or influence
2b) be established, become known, to come (fall) into or unto
3) to go, to follow one
For Synonyms see entry G5818
