שׁקק
shâqaq
shaw-kak'
A primitive root; to course (like a beast of prey); by implication to seek greedily
KJV Usage: have appetite, justle one against another, long, range, run (to and fro).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁקק

1. to run, run about, rush, run to and fro, be eager or greedy or thirsty
a. (Qal) roving, ranging, longing, rush out upon (participle)
b. (Hithpalpel) to rush to and fro, rush back and forth
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2460
Parts of Speech: Verb
