בּוּס
bûs
boos
A primitive root; to trample (literally or figuratively)
KJV Usage: loath, tread (down, under [foot]), be polluted.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

בּוּס

1. to tread down, reject, trample down
a. (Qal)
1. to tread down, trample (of warriors)
2. of rejection (fig.)
b. (Polel)
1. to tread down (in bad sense)
2. to desecrate
c. (Hithpolel)
1. to tread
2. to kick out 1c
d. of infants' blind movements 1c
e. of Jerusalem (fig.)
f. (Hophal) to be trodden down
