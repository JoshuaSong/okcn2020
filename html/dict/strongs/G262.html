ἀμαράντινος
of amaranth
ἀμαράντινος "amaranthine", i.e. (by implication) fadeless
Derivation: from G263;
KJV Usage: that fadeth not away. G263
Thayer:
1) composed of amaranth
1a) a flower so called because it never withers or fades, and when plucked off revives if moistened with water
1b) a symbol of perpetuity and immortality
Berry's New Testament Synonyms
  • Incorruptible, unfading
Trench's New Testament Synonyms
  • Incorruptible
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Fade (away)
