ἐκκλίνω
to bend out of the regular line
ἐκκλίνω to deviate, i.e. (absolutely) to shun (literally or figuratively), or (relatively) to decline (from piety)
Derivation: from G1537 and G2827;
KJV Usage: avoid, eschew, go out of the way. G1537 G2827
Thayer:
1) to turn aside, deviate (from the right way and course)
2) to turn (one's self) away, to turn away from, keep aloof from one's society
3) to shun one
