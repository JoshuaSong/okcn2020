כּבד כּבד
kâbad kâbêd
kaw-bad, kaw-bade'
A primitive root; to be heavy, that is, in a bad sense (burdensome, severe, dull) or in a good sense (numerous, rich, honorable); causatively to make weighty (in the same two senses)
KJV Usage: abounding with, more grievously afflict, boast, be chargeable, X be dim, glorify, be (make) glorious (things), glory, (very) great, be grievous, harden, be (make) heavy, be heavier, lay heavily, (bring to, come to, do, get, be had in) honour (self), (be) honourable (man), lade, X more be laid, make self many, nobles, prevail, promote (to honour), be rich, be (go) sore, stop.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּבד כּבד

1. to be heavy, be weighty, be grievous, be hard, be rich, be honourable, be glorious, be burdensome, be honoured
a. (Qal)
1. to be heavy
2. to be heavy, be insensible, be dull
3. to be honoured
b. (Niphal)
1. to be made heavy, be honoured, enjoy honour, be made abundant
2. to get oneself glory or honour, gain glory
c. (Piel)
1. to make heavy, make dull, make insensible
2. to make honourable, honour, glorify
d. (Pual) to be made honourable, be honoured
e. (Hiphil)
1. to make heavy
2. to make heavy, make dull, make unresponsive
3. to cause to be honoured
f. (Hithpael)
1. to make oneself heavy, make oneself dense, make oneself numerous
2. to honour oneself
Origin: a primitive root

Parts of Speech: Verb
