עוּד
‛ûd
ood
A primitive root; to duplicate or repeat; by implication to protest, testify (as by reiteration); intensively to encompass, restore (as a sort of reduplication)
KJV Usage: admonish, charge, earnestly, lift up, protest, call (take) to record, relieve, rob, solemnly, stand upright, testify, give warning, (bear, call to, give, take to) witness.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עוּד

1. to return, repeat, go about, do again
a. (Piel) to surround, go round and round
b. (Pilel) to restore, relieve
c. (Hithpalel) to be restored
2. to bear witness
a. (Qal) to bear witness, say again and again
b. (Hiphil)
1. to testify, bear witness
2. to cause to testify, take or call as witness, invoke
3. to protest, affirm solemnly, warn, exhort or enjoin solemnly, admonish, charge
c. (Hophal) to protest, give warning
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1576,1576d
Parts of Speech: Verb
