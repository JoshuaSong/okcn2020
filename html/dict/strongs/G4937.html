συντρίβω
to rub together
συντρίβω to crush completely, i.e. to shatter (literally or figuratively)
Derivation: from G4862 and the base of G5147;
KJV Usage: break (in pieces), broken to shivers (+ -hearted), bruise. G4862 G5147
Thayer:
1) break, to break in pieces, shiver
2) to tread down
2a) to put Satan under foot and (as a conqueror) trample on him
2b) to break down, crush
2b1) to tear one's body and shatter one's strength
