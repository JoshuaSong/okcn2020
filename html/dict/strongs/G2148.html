Εὐροκλύδων a storm from the East (or southeast), i.e. (in modern phrase) a Levanter
Derivation: from Εὖρος (the east wind) and G2830;
KJV Usage: Euroklydon. G2830
Thayer:
1) south east wind raising mighty waves
2) a wind causing broad waves
Literal: Euroclydon = "a violent agitation"
