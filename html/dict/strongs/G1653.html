ἐλεέω
to have pity on, shew mercy upon
ἐλεέω to compassionate (by word or deed, specially, by divine grace)
Derivation: from G1656;
KJV Usage: have compassion (pity on), have (obtain, receive, shew) mercy (on). G1656
Thayer:
1) to have mercy on
2) to help one afflicted or seeking aid
3) to help the afflicted, to bring help to the wretched
4) to experience mercy
For Synonyms see entry G5842
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Compassion, Compassionate
  • Merciful (Adjective, and Verb, to be), Mercy (Noun, and Verb, to have, etc.)
