μεταίρω
to lift up and remove, to shift
μεταίρω to betake oneself, i.e. remove (locally)
Derivation: from G3326 and G142;
KJV Usage: depart. G3326 G142
Thayer:
1) to lift up and remove from one place to another, to transfer
2) to go away, depart
