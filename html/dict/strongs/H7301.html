רוה
râvâh
raw-vaw'
A primitive root; to slake the thirst (occasionally of other appetites)
KJV Usage: bathe, make drunk, (take the) fill, satiate, (abundantly) satisfy, soak, water (abundantly).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

רוה

1. to be satiated or saturated, have or drink one's fill
a. (Qal) to take one's fill
b. (Piel)
1. to be drunk, be intoxicated
2. to drench, water abundantly, saturate
c. (Hiphil) to saturate, water, cause to drink
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2130
Parts of Speech: Verb
