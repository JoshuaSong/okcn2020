סוּג
sûg
soog
A primitive root; properly to flinch, that is, (by implication) to go back, literally (to retreat) or figuratively (to apostatize)
KJV Usage: backslider, drive, go back, turn (away, back).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

סוּג

1. to move, go, turn back, move away, backslide
a. (Qal) to backslide, prove recreant to
b. (Niphal)
1. to turn oneself away, turn back
2. to be turned or driven back, be repulsed
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1469
Parts of Speech: Verb
