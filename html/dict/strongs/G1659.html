ἐλευθερόω
to free, set free
ἐλευθερόω to liberate, i.e. (figuratively) to exempt (from moral, ceremonial or mortal liability)
Derivation: from G1658;
KJV Usage: deliver, make free. G1658
Thayer:
1) to make free
2) set at liberty: from the dominion of sin
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Deliver, Deliverance, Deliverer
  • Free, Freedom, Freely, Freeman, Freedman, Freewoman
