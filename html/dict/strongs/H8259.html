שׁקף
shâqaph
shaw-kaf'
A primitive root; properly to lean out (of a window), that is, (by implication) peep or gaze (passively be a spectacle)
KJV Usage: appear, look (down, forth, out).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁקף

1. to overlook, look down or out, overhang, look out and down
a. (Niphal) to lean over (and look), look down
b. (Hiphil) to look down, look down upon
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2457
Parts of Speech: Verb
