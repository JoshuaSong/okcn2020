ἐκφέρω
to carry out of
ἐκφέρω to bear out (literally or figuratively)
Derivation: from G1537 and G5342;
KJV Usage: bear, bring forth, carry forth (out). G1537 G5342
Thayer:
1) to carry out, to bear forth
1a) the dead for burial
2) to bring i.e. lead out
3) to bring forth, i.e. produce
3a) of the earth bearing plants
