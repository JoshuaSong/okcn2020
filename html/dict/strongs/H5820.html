‛azmâveth
az-maw'-veth
From H5794 and H4194; strong one of death; Azmaveth, the name of three Israelites and of a place in Palestine
KJV Usage: Azmaveth. See also H1041.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עזמות
Azmaveth = "strong unto death" n pr m
1. one of David's mighty warriors
2. a descendant of Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan and grandson of king Saul
3. a Benjamite, father of Jeziel and Pelet who were two of David's men who joined him at Ziklag
4. overseer of the royal treasures in the reign of David n pr loc
5. a place in Benjamin; also 'Beth-azmeveth'
Origin: from H5794 and H4194
TWOT: None
