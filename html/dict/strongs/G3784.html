ὀφείλω
to owe, have to pay
ὀφείλω , or (in certain tenses), its prolonged form ὀφειλέω to owe (pecuniarily); figuratively, to be under obligation (ought, must, should); morally, to fail in duty
Derivation: probably from the base of G3786 (through the idea of accruing);
KJV Usage: behove, be bound, (be) debt(-or), (be) due(-ty), be guilty (indebted), (must) need(-s), ought, owe, should. See also G3785. G3786 G3785
Thayer:
1) to owe
1a) to owe money, be in debt for
1a1) that which is due, the debt
2) metaph. the goodwill due
