דּרך
dârak
daw-rak'
A primitive root; to tread; by implication to walk; also to string a bow (by treading on it in bending)
KJV Usage: archer, bend, come, draw, go (over), guide, lead (forth), thresh, tread (down), walk.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

דּרך

1. to tread, bend, lead, march
a. (Qal)
1. to tread, march, march forth
2. to tread on, tread upon
3. to tread (a press)
4. to tread (bend) a bow
5. archer, bow-benders (participle)
b. (Hiphil)
1. to tread, tread down
2. to tread (bend with the foot) a bow
3. to cause to go, lead, march, tread
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 453
Parts of Speech: Verb
