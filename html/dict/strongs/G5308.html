ὑψηλός
high, lofty, high-raised
ὑψηλός lofty (in place or character)
Derivation: from G5311;
KJV Usage: high(-er, -ly) (esteemed). G5311
Thayer:
1) high, lofty
1a) exalted on high
1b) with an uplifted arm, i.e. with signal power
2) metaph. eminent, exalted
2a) in influence and honour
2b) to set the mind on, to seek, high things (as honours and riches), to be aspiring
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Exalt, Exalted
  • High (from on, Most), Highly
