πυνθάνομαι
to learn by hearsay
πυνθάνομαι to question, i.e. ascertain by inquiry (as a matter of information merely; and thus differing from G2065, which properly means a request as a favor; and from G154, which is strictly a demand for something due; as well as from G2212, which implies a search for something hidden; and from G1189, which involves the idea of urgent need); by implication, to learn (by casual intelligence)
Derivation: middle voice prolonged from a primary πύθω (which occurs only as an alternate in certain tenses);
KJV Usage: ask, demand, enquire, understand. G2065 G154 G2212 G1189
Thayer:
1) to enquire, ask
2) to ascertain, by enquiry
