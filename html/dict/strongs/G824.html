ἄτοπος
out of place
ἄτοπος out of place, i.e. (figuratively) improper, injurious, wicked
Derivation: from G1 (as a negative particle) and G5117;
KJV Usage: amiss, harm, unreasonable. G1 G5117
Thayer:
1) out of place, not befitting, unbecoming
2) improper, wicked
3) unrighteous
4) inconvenient, harmful
