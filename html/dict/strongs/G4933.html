συντηρέω
to preserve together
συντηρέω to keep closely together, i.e. (by implication) to conserve (from ruin); mentally, to remember (and obey)
Derivation: from G4862 and G5083;
KJV Usage: keep, observe, preserve. G4862 G5083
Thayer:
1) to preserve (a thing from perishing or being lost)
2) to keep within one's self, keep in mind (a thing, lest it be forgotten)
