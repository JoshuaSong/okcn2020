ξέστης
pint
ξέστης by implication, (of friction) to boil or heat); a vessel (as fashioned or for cooking) (or perhaps by corruption from the Latin sextarius, the sixth of a modius, i.e. about a pint), i.e. (specially), a measure for liquids or solids, (by analogy, a pitcher)
Derivation: as if from ξέω (properly, to smooth;
KJV Usage: pot.
Thayer:
1) a sextarius
1a) a vessel for measuring liquids, holding about a pint (.
5 litre)
2) a wooden pitcher or ewer from which water or wine is poured, whether holding a sextarius or not
