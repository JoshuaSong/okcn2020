κακοπαθέω
to suffer ill, to be in ill plight, be in distress
κακοπαθέω to undergo hardship
Derivation: from the same as G2552;
KJV Usage: be afflicted, endure afflictions (hardness), suffer trouble. G2552
Thayer:
1) to suffer (endure) evils (hardships, troubles)
2) to be afflicted
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Afflict (-ed), Affliction
  • Hardship (to suffer)
