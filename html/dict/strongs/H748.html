ארך
'ârak
aw-rak'
A primitive root; to be (causatively make) long (literally or figuratively)
KJV Usage: defer, draw out, lengthen, (be, become, make, pro-) long, + (out-, over-) live, tarry (long).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

ארך

1. to be long, prolong
a. (Qal) to be long
b. (Hiphil)
1. to prolong (days)
2. to make long (tent cords)
3. to grow long, continue long
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 162
Parts of Speech: Verb
