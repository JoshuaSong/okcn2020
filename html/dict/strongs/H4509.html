minyâmı̂yn
min-yaw-meen'
From H4480 and H3225; from (the) right hand; Minjamin, the name of two Israelites
KJV Usage: Miniamin. Compare H4326.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

מנימין
Miniamin = "from the right hand"
1. a Levite in the time of Hezekiah
2. a priest who was present at the dedication of the wall of Jerusalem in the time of Nehemiah
Origin: from H4480 and H3225
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
