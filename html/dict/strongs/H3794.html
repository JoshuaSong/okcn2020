כּתּיּי כּתּי
kittı̂y kittı̂yı̂y
kit-tee', kit-tee-ee'
Patrial from an unused name denoting Cyprus (only in the plural); a Kittite or Cypriote; hence an islander in general, that is, the Greeks or Romans on the shores opposite Palestine
KJV Usage: Chittim, Kittim.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּתּיּי כּתּי
Chittim or Kittim = "bruisers"
1. a general term for all islanders of the Mediterranean Sea
2. the descendants of Javan, the son of Japheth and grandson of Noah
Origin: patrial from an unused name denoting Cyprus (only in the pl.)
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Adjective
