עכר
‛âkar
aw-kar'
A primitive root; properly to roil water; figuratively to disturb or afflict
KJV Usage: trouble, stir.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עכר

1. to trouble, stir up, disturb, make (someone) taboo
a. (Qal) to disturb, trouble
b. (Niphal)
1. to be disturbed, be stirred up
2. disturbance, calamity (participle)
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1621
Parts of Speech: Verb
