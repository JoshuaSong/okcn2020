διχοτομέω
to cut in two, cut in twain
διχοτομέω to bisect, i.e. (by extension) to flog severely
Derivation: from a compound of a derivative of G1364 and a derivative of τέμνω (to cut);
KJV Usage: cut asunder (in sunder). G1364
Thayer:
1) to cut into two parts
1a) of the cruel method of punishment used by the Hebrews and others of cutting one in two
2) cut up by scourging, scourge severely
