עתר
‛âthar
aw-thar'
A primitive root (rather denominative from H6281); to burn incense in worship, that is, intercede (reciprocally listen to prayer)
KJV Usage: intreat, (make) pray (-er).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עתר

1. to pray, entreat, supplicate
a. (Qal) to pray, entreat
b. (Niphal) to be supplicated, be entreated
c. (Hiphil) to make supplication, plead
Origin: a primitive root [rather denominative from H6281]
TWOT: 1722
Parts of Speech: Verb
