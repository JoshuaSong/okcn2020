εἰσπορεύομαι to enter (literally or figuratively)
Derivation: from G1519 and G4198;
KJV Usage: come (enter) in, go into. G1519 G4198
Thayer:
1) to go into, enter
1a) of persons
1b) of things
1b1) to be carried into or put into
1b2) as food into a mouth
2) metaph. of affections entering the soul
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Enter, Entering, Entrance
  • Go (went), Go onward, etc.
