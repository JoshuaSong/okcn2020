κοιμάω
to lull
κοιμάω to put to sleep, i.e. (passively or reflexively) to slumber; figuratively, to decease
Derivation: from G2749;
KJV Usage: (be a-, fall a-, fall on) sleep, be dead. G2749
Thayer:
1) to cause to sleep, put to sleep
2) metaph.
2a) to still, calm, quiet
2b) to fall asleep, to sleep
2c) to die
