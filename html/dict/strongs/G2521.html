κάθημαι
to be seated
κάθημαι and ἧμαι (to sit; akin to the base of G1476); to sit down; figuratively, to remain, reside
Derivation: from G2596;
KJV Usage: dwell, sit (by, down). G2596 G1476
Thayer:
1) to sit down, seat one's self
2) to sit, be seated, of a place occupied
2a) to have a fixed abode, to dwell
