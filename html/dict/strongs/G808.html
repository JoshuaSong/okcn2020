ἀσχημοσύνη
want of form, ungracefulness
ἀσχημοσύνη an indecency; by implication, the pudenda
Derivation: from G809;
KJV Usage: shame, that which is unseemly. G809
Thayer:
ἀσχημοσύνη, -ης, ἡ, (ἀσχήμων); fr. Plato down; unseemliness, an unseemly deed: Ro. i. 27; of the pudenda, one's nakedness, shame: Rev. xvi. 15, as in Ex. xx. 26; Deut. xxiii. 14, etc. (In Grk. writ. fr. Plat. down.)*
1) unseemliness, an unseemly deed
1a) of a woman's genitals
1b) of one's nakedness, shame
