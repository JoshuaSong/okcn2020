כּפתּור כּפתּר
kaphtôr kaphtôr
kaf-tore', kaf-tore'
Probably from an unused root meaning to encircle; a chaplet; but used only in an architectonic sense, that is, the capital of a column, or a wreath like button or disk on the candelabrum
KJV Usage: knop, (upper) lintel.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּפתּור כּפתּר

1. bulb, knob, capital, capital of a pillar
a. knob, bulb (as ornament)
b. capital (of a pillar)
Origin: probably from an unused root meaning to encircle
