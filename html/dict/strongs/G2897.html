κραιπάλη
a drunken head-ache
κραιπάλη properly, a headache (as a seizure of pain) from drunkenness, i.e. (by implication) a debauch (by analogy, a glut)
Derivation: probably from the same as G726;
KJV Usage: surfeiting. G726
Thayer:
1) the giddiness and headache caused by drinking wine to excess

  • Drunkenness, drinking
Trench's New Testament Synonyms

Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
