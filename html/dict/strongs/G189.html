ἀκοή
a hearing, the sound heard
ἀκοή hearing (the act, the sense or the thing heard)
Derivation: from G191;
KJV Usage: audience, ear, fame, which ye heard, hearing, preached, report, rumor. G191
Thayer:
1) the sense of hearing
2) the organ of hearing, the ear
3) the thing heard
3a) instruction, namely oral
3a1) of preaching the gospel
3b) hearsay, report or rumour
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words

  • Hear, Hearing
  • Message
  • Report (Noun and Verb)
  • Rumor
