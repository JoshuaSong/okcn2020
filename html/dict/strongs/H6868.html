tse rêdâh tse rêdâthâh
tser-ay-daw', tser-ay-daw'-thaw
Apparently from an unused root meaning to pierce; puncture; Tseredah, a place in Palestine
KJV Usage: Zereda, Zeredathah.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

צרדתה צרדה
Zereda or Zeredathah = "fortress"
1. a town or territory in Manasseh and the home of Jeroboam
Origin: apparently from an unused root meaning to pierce
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Location
