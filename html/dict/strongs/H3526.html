כּבס
kâbas
kaw-bas'
A primitive root; to trample; hence to wash (properly by stamping with the feet), whether literally (including the fulling process) or figuratively
KJV Usage: fuller, wash (-ing).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּבס

1. to wash (by treading), be washed, perform the work of a fuller
a. (Qal) washer, fuller, treader (participle)
b. (Piel) to wash (garments, person)
c. (Pual) to be washed
d. (Hothpael) to be washed out
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 946
Parts of Speech: Verb
