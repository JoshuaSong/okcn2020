σινιάζω
to sift, winnow
σινιάζω to riddle (figuratively)
Derivation: from σινίον (a sieve);
KJV Usage: sift.
Thayer:
1) to sift, shake in a sieve
2) fig. by inward agitation to try one's faith to the verge of overthrow
