ὀπή
an opening, hole
ὀπή a hole (as if for light), i.e. cavern; by analogy, a spring (of water)
Derivation: probably from G3700;
KJV Usage: cave, place. G3700
Thayer:
1) through which one can see, an opening, an aperture
1a) of a window
1b) of fissures in the earth
1c) of caves in rocks or mountains, holes
