קצע
qâtsa‛
kaw-tsah'
A primitive root; to strip off, that is, (partially) scrape; by implication to segregate (as an angle)
KJV Usage: cause to scrape, corner.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קצע

1. to scrape, scrape off, cut off
a. (Hiphil) to scrape
2. to be cornered, be set in corners
a. (Hophal) to cut off places, make corners
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2056,2057
Parts of Speech: Verb
