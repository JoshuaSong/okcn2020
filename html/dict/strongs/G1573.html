ἐκκακέω
to be faint-hearted
ἐκκακέω to be (bad or) weak, i.e. (by implication) to fail (in heart)
Derivation: from G1537 and G2556;
KJV Usage: faint, be weary. G1537 G2556
Thayer:
1) to be utterly spiritless, to be wearied out, exhausted
