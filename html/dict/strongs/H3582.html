כּחד
kâchad
kaw-khad'
A primitive root; to secrete, by act or word; hence (intensively) to destroy
KJV Usage: conceal, cut down (off), desolate, hide.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

כּחד

1. to hide, conceal, cut off, cut down, make desolate, kick
a. (Niphal)
1. to be hidden
2. to be effaced, be destroyed, be cut off
b. (Piel) to cover, hide
c. (Hiphil)
1. to hide
2. to efface, annihilate
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 972
Parts of Speech: Verb
