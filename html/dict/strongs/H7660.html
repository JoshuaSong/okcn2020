שׁבץ
shâbats
shaw-bats'
A primitive root; to interweave (colored) threads in squares; by implication (of reticulation) to inchase gems in gold
KJV Usage: embroider, set.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁבץ

1. to weave in checkered or plaited work or pattern (meaning probable)
a. (Piel) to weave, plait
b. (Pual) inwoven, set (participle)
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2320
Parts of Speech: Verb
