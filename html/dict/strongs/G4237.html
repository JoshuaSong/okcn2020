πρασιά
a bed of leeks
πρασιά a garden plot, i.e. (by implication, of regular beds) a row (repeated in plural by Hebraism, to indicate an arrangement)
Derivation: perhaps from πράσον (a leek, and so an onion-patch);
KJV Usage: in ranks.
Thayer:
1) a plot of ground, a garden bed
2) Hebrew idiom i.e. they reclined in ranks or divisions, so that several ranks formed, as it were separate plots
