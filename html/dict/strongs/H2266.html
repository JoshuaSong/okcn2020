חבר
châbar
khaw-bar'
A primitive root; to join (literally or figuratively); specifically (by means of spells) to fascinate
KJV Usage: charm (-er), be compact, couple (together), have fellowship with, heap up, join (self, together), league.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

חבר

1. to unite, join, bind together, be joined, be coupled, be in league, heap up, have fellowship with, be compact, be a charmer
a. (Qal)
1. to unite, be joined
2. to tie magic charms, charm
b. (Piel)
1. to unite with, make an ally of
2. to unite, join, ally
c. (Pual)
1. to be allied with, be united
2. to be joined together
d. (Hiphil) to join together, pile up (words)
e. (Hithpael) to join oneself to, make an alliance, league together
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 598
Parts of Speech: Verb
