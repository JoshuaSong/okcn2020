עקר
‛âqar
aw-kar'
A primitive root; to pluck up (especially by the roots); specifically to hamstring; figuratively to exterminate
KJV Usage: dig down, hough, pluck up, root up.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עקר

1. to pluck up, root up
a. (Qal) to pluck up, root up
b. (Niphal) to be plucked up
2. to cut, hamstring
a. (Piel) to cut, hamstring
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1681,1682
Parts of Speech: Verb
