פּחד
pâchad
paw-khad'
A primitive root; to be startled (by a sudden alarm); hence to fear in general
KJV Usage: be afraid, stand in awe, (be in) fear, make to shake.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

פּחד

1. to fear, tremble, revere, dread, be in awe or dread
a. (Qal)
1. to be in dread
2. to be in awe
b. (Piel) to be in great dread
c. (Hiphil) to cause to dread
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1756
Parts of Speech: Verb
