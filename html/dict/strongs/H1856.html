דּקר
dâqar
daw-kar'
A primitive root; to stab; by analogy to starve; figuratively to revile
KJV Usage: pierce, strike (thrust) through, wound.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

דּקר

1. to pierce, thrust through, pierce through
a. (Qal) to pierce, run through
b. (Niphal) to be pierced through
c. (Pual) pierced, riddled (participle)
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 449
Parts of Speech: Verb
