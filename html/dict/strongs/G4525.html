σαίνω
to wag the tail, fawn
σαίνω to wag (as a dog its tail fawningly), i.e. (generally) to shake (figuratively, disturb)
Derivation: akin to G4579;
KJV Usage: move. G4579
Thayer:
1) to wag the tail
1a) of dogs
2) metaph.
2a) to flatter, fawn upon
2b) to move (the mind of one)
2b1) agreeably
2b2) to agitate, disturb, trouble
