זנח
zânach
zaw-nakh'
A primitive root meaning to push aside, that is, reject, forsake, fail
KJV Usage: cast away (off), remove far away (off).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

זנח

1. to cast off, reject, spurn
a. (Qal) to reject
b. (Hiphil) to forcefully reject someone
2. to stink, emit stench, become odious
a. (Hiphil) stink (perfect)
Origin: a primitive root meaning to push aside
TWOT: 564
Parts of Speech: Verb
