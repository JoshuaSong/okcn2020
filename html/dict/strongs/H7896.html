שׁית
shı̂yth
sheeth
A primitive root; to place (in a very wide application)
KJV Usage: apply, appoint, array, bring, consider, lay (up), let alone, X look, make, mark, put (on), + regard, set, shew, be stayed, X take.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁית

1. to put, set
a. (Qal)
1. to put, lay (hand upon)
2. to set, station, appoint, fix, set mind to
3. to constitute, make (one something), make like, perform
4. to take one's stand
5. to lay waste
b. (Hophal) to be imposed, be set upon
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2380
Parts of Speech: Verb
