form
ἰδέα a sight (comparative figuratively "idea"), i.e. aspect
Derivation: from G1492;
KJV Usage: countenance. G1492
Thayer:
ἰδέα, -ας, ἡ, (fr. εἶδον, ἰδεῖν), form, external appearance; aspect, look: Mt. xxviii. 3 (T Tr WH εἰδέα, q. v.), cf. Alberti, Observv. ad loc.; [Tdf. Proleg. p. 81]. (Grk. writ. fr. Pind. and Hdt. down; 2 Macc. iii. 16; for דְּמזּת Gen. v. 3.) [Cf. Schmidt ch. 182, 3.]*
1) form, external appearance
2) aspect, look
