καθεύδω
to lie down to sleep, sleep
καθεύδω to lie down to rest, i.e. (by implication) to fall asleep (literally or figuratively)
Derivation: from G2596 and εὕδω (to sleep);
KJV Usage: (be a-)sleep. G2596
Thayer:
1) to fall asleep, drop off to sleep
2) to sleep
2a) to sleep normally
2b) euphemistically, to be dead
2c) metaph.
2c1) to yield to sloth and sin
2c2) to be indifferent to one's salvation
