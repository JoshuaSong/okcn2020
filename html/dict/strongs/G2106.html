εὐδοκέω
to be well pleased
εὐδοκέω to think well of, i.e. approve (an act); specially, to approbate (a person or thing)
Derivation: from G2095 and G1380;
KJV Usage: think good, (be well) please(-d), be the good (have, take) pleasure, be willing. G2095 G1380
Thayer:
1) it seems good to one, is one's good pleasure
1a) think it good, choose, determine, decide
1b) to do willingly
1c) to be ready to, to prefer, choose rather
2) to be well pleased with, take pleasure in, to be favourably inclined towards one
