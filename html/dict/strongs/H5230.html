נכל
nâkal
naw-kal'
A primitive root; to defraud, that is, act treacherously
KJV Usage: beguile, conspire, deceiver, deal subtilly.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

נכל

1. to be deceitful, be crafty, be knavish
a. (Qal)
1. to be knavish
2. deceiver (participle)
b. (Piel) to beguile
c. (Hithpael) to act craftily, act knavishly, deal knavishly
Origin: a primary root
TWOT: 1366
Parts of Speech: Verb
