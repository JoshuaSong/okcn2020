קדמה קדם
qedem qêdmâh
keh'-dem, kayd'-maw
From H6923; the front, of palce (absolutely the fore part, relatively the East) or time (antiquity); often used adverbially (before, anciently, eastward)
KJV Usage: aforetime, ancient (time), before, east (end, part, side, -ward), eternal, X ever (-lasting), forward, old, past. Compare H6926.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

קדמה קדם
n m
1. east, antiquity, front, that which is before, aforetime
a. front, from the front or east, in front, mount of the East
b. ancient time, aforetime, ancient, from of old, earliest time
c. anciently, of old (adverb)
d. beginning
e. east adv
2. eastward, to or toward the East
Origin: from H6923
TWOT: 1988a
