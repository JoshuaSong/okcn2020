תּקן
tâqan
taw-kan'
A primitive root; to equalize, that is, straighten (intransitively or transitively); figuratively to compose
KJV Usage: set in order, make straight.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

תּקן

1. to equalise, make straight, become straight
a. (Qal) to become straight
b. (Piel) to make straight, put straight, arrange in order, set in order
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 2540
Parts of Speech: Verb
