ἀδικέω
to do wrong
ἀδικέω to be unjust, i.e. (actively) do wrong (morally, socially or physically)
Derivation: from G94;
KJV Usage: hurt, injure, be an offender, be unjust, (do, suffer, take) wrong. G94
Thayer:
1) absolutely
1a) to act unjustly or wickedly, to sin,
1b) to be a criminal, to have violated the laws in some way
1c) to do wrong
1d) to do hurt
2) transitively
2a) to do some wrong or sin in some respect
2b) to wrong some one, act wickedly towards him
2c) to hurt, damage, harm
