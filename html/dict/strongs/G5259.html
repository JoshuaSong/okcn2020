ὑπό
from under, by, c. gen. under, c. dat., towards c. acc.
ὑπό under, i.e. (with the genitive case) of place (beneath), or with verbs (the agency or means, through); (with the accusative case) of place (whither (underneath) or where (below) or time (when (at))
Derivation: a primary preposition;
KJV Usage: among, by, from, in, of, under, with. In the comparative, it retains the same general applications, especially of inferior position or condition, and specially, covertly or moderately.
Thayer:
1) by, under
