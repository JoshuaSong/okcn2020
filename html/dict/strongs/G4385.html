προτείνω
to stretch out before, hold before
προτείνω to protend, i.e. tie prostrate (for scourging)
Derivation: from G4253 and τείνω (to stretch);
KJV Usage: bind. G4253
Thayer:
1) to stretch forth, stretch out
1a) when they had stretched one out on the thongs i.e. to receive the blows of the thongs (by tying him up to a beam or a pillar)
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Bind, Binding (see also Bound)
  • Tie
