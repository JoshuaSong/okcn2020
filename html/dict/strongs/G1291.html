διαστέλλομαι to set (oneself) apart (figuratively, distinguish), i.e. (by implication) to enjoin
Derivation: middle voice from G1223 and G4724;
KJV Usage: charge, that which was (give) commanded(-ment). G1223 G4724
Thayer:
1) to draw asunder, divide, distinguish, dispose, order
2) to open one's self i.e. one's mind, to set forth distinctly
3) to admonish, order, charge
