παρά
from the side of, c. gen., beside, alongside of, c. dat., to the side of, motion alongside of, c. acc.
παρά properly, near; i.e. (with genitive case) from beside (literally or figuratively), (with dative case) at (or in) the vicinity of (objectively or subjectively), (with accusative case) to the proximity with (local (especially beyond or opposed to) or causal (on account of)
Derivation: a primary preposition;
KJV Usage: above, against, among, at, before, by, contrary to, X friend, from, + give (such things as they), + that (she) had, X his, in, more than, nigh unto, (out) of, past, save, side…by, in the sight of, than, (there-)fore, with. In compounds it retains the same variety of application.
Thayer:
1) from, of at, by, besides, near
