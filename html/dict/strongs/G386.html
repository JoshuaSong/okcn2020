ἀνάστασις
a raising up
ἀνάστασις a standing up again, i.e. (literally) a resurrection from death (individual, genitive case or by implication, (its author)), or (figuratively) a (moral) recovery (of spiritual truth)
Derivation: from G450;
KJV Usage: raised to life again, resurrection, rise from the dead, that should rise, rising again. G450
Thayer:
1) a raising up, rising (e.g. from a seat)
2) a rising from the dead
2a) that of Christ
2b) that of all men at the end of this present age
2c) the resurrection of certain ones history who were restored to life (Heb.
11:35)
