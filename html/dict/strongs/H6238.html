עשׁר
‛âshar
aw-shar'
A primitive root; properly to accumulate; chiefly (specifically) to grow (causatively make) rich
KJV Usage: be (-come, en-, make, make self, wax) rich, make [H1 Kings H22 : H48 margin]. See H6240.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

עשׁר

1. to be or become rich or wealthy, enrich, pretend to be rich
a. (Qal) to be or become rich
b. (Hiphil)
1. to make rich
2. to gain riches
c. (Hithpael) to enrich oneself, pretend to be rich
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1714
Parts of Speech: Verb
