δύσκολος
hard to satisfy with food;
δύσκολος properly, fastidious about eating (peevish), i.e. (genitive case) impracticable
Derivation: from G1418 and κόλον (food);
KJV Usage: hard. G1418
Thayer:
δύσκολος, -ον, (κόλον food);
1. prop. hard to find agreeable food for, fastidious about food.
2. difficult to please, always finding fault; (Eur., Arstph., Xen., Plat., al.).
3. univ. difficult (Xen. oec. 15, 10 ἡ γεωργία δύσκολός ἐστι μαθεῖν): πῶς δύσκολόν ἐστι, foll. by acc. with inf., Mk. x. 24.*
1) hard to find agreeable food for, fastidious about food
