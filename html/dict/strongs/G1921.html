ἐπιγινώσκω to know upon some mark, i.e. recognize; by implication, to become fully acquainted with, to acknowledge
Derivation: from G1909 and G1097;
KJV Usage: (ac-, have, take)know(-ledge, well), perceive. G1909 G1097
Thayer:
1) to become thoroughly acquainted with, to know thoroughly
1a) to know accurately, know well
2) to know
2a) to recognise
2a1) by sight, hearing, of certain signs, to perceive who a person is
2b) to know i.e. to perceive
2c) to know i.e. to find out, ascertain
2d) to know i.e. to understand
