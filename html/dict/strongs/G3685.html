ὀνίνημι
to profit, benefit, help, assist
ὀνίνημι for which another prolonged form (ὀνάω) is used as an alternate in some tenses (unless indeed it be identical with the base of G3686 through the idea of notoriety); to gratify, i.e. (middle voice) to derive pleasure or advantage from
Derivation: a prolonged form of an apparently primary verb (ὄνομαι, to slur);
KJV Usage: have joy. G3686
Thayer:
1) to be useful, to profit, help
2) to receive profit or advantage, be helped [or have joy]
