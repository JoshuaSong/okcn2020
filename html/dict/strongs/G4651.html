σκορπίος
a scorpion
σκορπίος a "scorpion" (from its sting)
Derivation: probably from an obsolete σκέρπω (perhaps strengthened from the base of G4649 and meaning to pierce);
KJV Usage: scorpion. G4649
Thayer:
1) a scorpion, the name of a little animal, somewhat resembling a lobster, which in warm regions lurk, esp. in stone walls; it has a poisonous sting in its tail
