סיעהא סיעא
sı̂y‛â' sı̂y‛ăhâ'
see-ah', see-ah-haw'
From an unused root meaning to converse; congregation; Sia, or Siaha, one of the Nethinim
KJV Usage: Sia, Siaha.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

סיעהא סיעא
Sia or Siaha = "departing"
1. ancestor of a family of temple slaves who returned from exile with Zerubbabel
Origin: from an unused root meaning to converse
TWOT: None
Parts of Speech: Proper Name Masculine
