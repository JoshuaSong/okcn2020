ἀνίημι
to send up
ἀνίημι to let up, i.e. (literally) slacken or (figuratively) desert, desist from
Derivation: from G303 and ἵημι (to send);
KJV Usage: forbear, leave, loose. G303
Thayer:
1) to send back, relax, loosen
2) to give up, omit, calm
3) to leave, not to uphold, to let sink
