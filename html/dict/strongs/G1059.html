Γαμαλιήλ Gamaliel (i.e. Gamliel), an Israelite
Derivation: of Hebrew origin (H1583);
KJV Usage: Gamaliel. H1583
Thayer:
1) a Pharisee and celebrated doctor of the law, who gave prudent worldly advice in the Sanhedrin respecting the treatment of the followers of Jesus of Nazareth. Acts
5:34 ff. (A.D.29.) We learn from Acts 22:3 that he was the preceptor of Paul. He is generally identified with the very celebrated Jewish doctor Gamaliel, grandson of Hillel, and who is referred to as authority in the Jewish Mishna.
Literal: Gamaliel = "my recompenser is God"
