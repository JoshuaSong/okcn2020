תּרוּעה
te rû‛âh
ter-oo-aw'
From H7321; clamor, that is, acclamation of joy or a battle cry; especially clangor of trumpets, as an alarum
KJV Usage: alarm, blow (-ing) (of, the) (trumpets), joy, jubile, loud noise, rejoicing, shout (-ing), (high, joyful) sound (-ing).

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

תּרוּעה

1. alarm, signal, sound of tempest, shout, shout or blast of war or alarm or joy
a. alarm of war, war-cry, battle-cry
b. blast (for march)
c. shout of joy (with religious impulse)
d. shout of joy (in general)
Origin: from H7321
TWOT: 2135b
Parts of Speech: Noun Feminine
