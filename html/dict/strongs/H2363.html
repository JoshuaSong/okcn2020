חוּשׁ
chûsh
koosh
A primitive root; to hurry; figuratively to be eager with excitement or enjoyment
KJV Usage: (make) haste (-n), ready.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

חוּשׁ

1. to haste, make haste, hurry
a. (Qal) to make haste
b. (Hiphil)
1. to show haste, act quickly, hasten, come quickly
2. to enjoy, be excited
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 631
Parts of Speech: Verb
