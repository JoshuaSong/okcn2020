στενοχωρέω
to straiten for room
στενοχωρέω to hem in closely, i.e. (figuratively) cramp
Derivation: from the same as G4730;
KJV Usage: distress, straiten. G4730
Thayer:
1) to be in a narrow place
2) to straiten, compress, cramp, reduce to straits
2a) to be sorely straitened in spirit
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words

  • Distress, Distressed
  • Strait (be in a), Straitened
