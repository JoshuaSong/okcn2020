ינשׁוף ינשׁוּף
yanshûph yanshôph
yan-shoof', yan-shofe'
Apparently from H4398; an unclean (aquatic) bird; probably the heron (perhaps from its blowing cry, or because the night heron is meant (compare H5399))
KJV Usage: (great) owl.
