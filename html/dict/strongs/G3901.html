παραῤῥυέω to flow by, i.e. (figuratively) carelessly pass (miss)
Derivation: from G3844 and the alternate of G4482;
KJV Usage: let slip. G3844 G4482
Thayer:
1) to glide by: lest we be carried by, pass by
1a) lest the salvation which these things heard show us how to obtain, slip away from us
1b) a thing escapes me
1c) slips my mind
