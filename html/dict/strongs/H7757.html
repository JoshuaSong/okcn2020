שׁוּל
shûl
shool
From an unused root meaning to hang down; a skirt; by implication a bottom edge
KJV Usage: hem, skirt, train.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

שׁוּל

1. skirt (of robe)
a. of high priest's robe
b. of God's train, city as woman, ignominy, defilement (fig)
Origin: from an unused root meaning to hang down
TWOT: 2346a
Parts of Speech: Noun Masculine
