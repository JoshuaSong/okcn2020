ἀποκτείνω
to kill, slay
ἀποκτείνω to kill outright; figuratively, to destroy
Derivation: from G575 and κτείνω (to slay);
KJV Usage: put to death, kill, slay. G575
Thayer:
1) to kill in any way whatever
1a) to destroy, to allow to perish
2) metaph. to extinguish, abolish
2a) to inflict mortal death
2b) to deprive of spiritual life and procure eternal misery in hell
Vine's Expository Dictionary of New Testament Words
  • Death, Death-stroke (see also Die)
  • Kill
  • Slay, Slain, Slew
