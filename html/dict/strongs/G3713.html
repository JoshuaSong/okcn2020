ὀρέγομαι to stretch oneself, i.e. reach out after (long for)
Derivation: middle voice of apparently a prolonged form of an obsolete primary (compare G3735);
KJV Usage: covet after, desire. G3735
Thayer:
1) to stretch one's self out in order to touch or to grasp something, to reach after or desire something
2) to give one's self up to the love of money
