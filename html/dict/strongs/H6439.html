פּניאל פּנוּאל
pe nû'êl pe nı̂y'êl
pen-oo-ale', pen-ee-ale'
From H6437 and H410; face of God; Penuel or Peniel, a place East of Jordan; also (as Penuel) the name of two Israelites
KJV Usage: Peniel, Penuel.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

פּניאל פּנוּאל
Penuel or Peniel = "facing God" n pr m
1. a Benjamite, son of Shashak, brother of Iphedeiah of the family of Saul
2. son of Hur, father of Gedor, and a descendant of Judah n pr loc
3. the place named by Jacob when he wrestled with God and located on the north bank of the Jabbok close to the Jordan
Origin: from H6437 and H410
TWOT: None
