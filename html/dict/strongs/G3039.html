λικμάω
to part the grain from the chaff, to winnow
λικμάω to winnow, i.e. (by analogy), to triturate
Derivation: from λικμός, the equivalent of λίκνον (a winnowing fan or basket);
KJV Usage: grind to powder.
Thayer:
1) to winnow, cleanse away the chaff from the grain by winnowing
2) to scatter
3) to crush to pieces, grind to powder
