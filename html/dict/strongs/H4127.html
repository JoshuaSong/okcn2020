מוּג
mûg
moog
A primitive root; to melt, that is, literally (to soften, flow down, disappear), or figuratively (to fear, faint)
KJV Usage: consume, dissolve, (be) faint (-hearted), melt (away), make soft.

Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions

מוּג

1. to melt, cause to melt
a. (Qal)
1. to melt, faint
2. to cause to melt
b. (Niphal) to melt away
c. (Polel) to soften, dissolve, dissipate
d. (Hithpolel) to melt, flow
Origin: a primitive root
TWOT: 1156
Parts of Speech: Verb
