σοφίζω
to make wise, instruct
σοφίζω to render wise; in a sinister acceptation, to form "sophisms", i.e. continue plausible error
Derivation: from G4680;
KJV Usage: cunningly devised, make wise. G4680
Thayer:
1) to make wise, teach
2) to become wise, to have understanding
2a) to invent, play the sophist
2b) to devise cleverly or cunningly
