συγχέω
to pour together, commingle, confound
συγχέω or συγχύνω to commingle promiscuously, i.e. (figuratively) to throw (an assembly) into disorder, to perplex (the mind)
Derivation: from G4862 and χέω (to pour) or its alternate;
KJV Usage: confound, confuse, stir up, be in an uproar. G4862
Thayer:
1) to pour together, commingle
2) to disturb the mind of one, to stir up to tumult or outbreak
3) to confound or bewilder
