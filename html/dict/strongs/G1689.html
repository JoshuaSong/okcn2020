ἐμβλέπω
to look in the face, look at
ἐμβλέπω to look on, i.e. (relatively) to observe fixedly, or (absolutely) to discern clearly
Derivation: from G1722 and G991;
KJV Usage: behold, gaze up, look upon, (could) see. G1722 G991
Thayer:
1) to turn one's eyes on
2) look at
3) metaph. to look at with the mind, to consider
